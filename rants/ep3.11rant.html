(This ended up going in a completely different direction than what I had originally written last night while tired and in need of sleep. I think it came out a lot better. Late updates just mean better quality.)

____ On September 16, a trail cam set up in the Allegheny National Forest in western Pennsylvania snapped a picture of two bear cubs investigating the mineral block that had been set out to attract deer. Twenty-eight minutes later, this was photographed twice. The Pennsylvania Game Commission insists it’s just a mangy bear while the Bradford Era newspaper isn’t so sure, and others leap right to the label of sasquatch.

____ The BFRO has a nice conspiracy theory about the Pennsylvania Game Commission and the photo the PGC provided of a mange stricken bear doesn’t really help their argument for the creature being a common bruin, so maybe they are trying to deny the obvious. Then again, the picture offered up by cryptozoologist Loren Coleman does seem to argue in favor of a skinny bear as opposed to an unknown species. Maybe if the animal was standing upright there’d be a better case for a sasquatch. Sarcoptic mange seems to be a common cause of cryptid sightings in recent years though, particularly in the chupacabras that keep getting killed in Texas. Disappointingly, they always turn out to be mangy coyotes.

____ While no mystery is best left unsolved, the identity of the Jacobs creature is probably going to remain unverifiable unless someone finds a mangy bear in the area very soon. At least we have the story revolving around it though, and in the absence of a new species of large mammal roaming Pennsylvania, we have the apophenia a strange photo has generated. Everything we do in life involves storytelling, and it takes bizarre incidents like this to make that apparent. There are people who “know” the animal is just a bear and there are those who “know” it is a sasquatch, and there are those who are not so sure they know what it is. While agnosticism seems to be prevailing in a lot of people surroundings these photographs, it doesn’t so much in less strange matters. I feel it should. We perceive the world through our expectations, and usually only remember the things which match our conception of how things “are.” When people repeat what others have said, they tend to misquote, only repeating the same general idea and not the exact phrasing. But they also have tendencies to alter even the root meaning, speaking what they thought was meant.

____ Everything we experience is essentially a photograph of a blurry animal. All we can do is interpret it through our filters and reality tunnels, only we don’t realize such and believe our perceptions of things to be the things in and of themselves. The sole reason for the lack of understanding in this world is the failure to realize that our ideas are not absolutes. Other people seem a lot less crazy when you understand that all of life is a fiction, and it is the molding of this fiction that gives our lives meaning. It is only when confronting the outré that this attribute of the human mind becomes apparent, whether it’s when a sick coyote turns into the blood sucking, escaped alien pet which terrorizes the southwest, or a blob of light becomes the Virgin Mary.

____ The Jacobs creature is the symbol of all our attempts at understanding reality and each other: at best, vague and confusing, at worst, “causing more concern and panic than is necessary,” to use the words of the Pennsylvania Game Commission. Maybe someone just needs to cartoon the beast up and slap him on a t-shirt like they did with the chupacabras, and he won’t be so scary anymore. ;)
