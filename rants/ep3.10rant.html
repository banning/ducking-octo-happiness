____ The spread of information technology is usually cited as one of the primary causes of the collapse of the Soviet Union. When the people saw how the rest of the world was living, they grew dissatisfied with their government. Transhumanists continue to espouse the praises of information technology as making government more open and revealing corruption, allowing our societies to become freer. So why then can the president declare any American citizen an enemy combatant, strip them of constitutional rights, and send them indefinitely to a European prison without telling anyone, not even the accused, of the crime committed? Or why has Congress passed a law violating the Constitution by legalizing warrantless wiretapping of American citizens? The nations of the Eastern Bloc conducted these same practices. The difference now is, we’re aware of it. We know which rooms in which telecommunication buildings the NSA has installed espionage technology. We know that the United States government is torturing prisoners, where it is torturing them, and what techniques are being used. Hell, we know that, prior to taking power, the Vice President, the former Secretary of Defense, and the former President of the World Bank Group, among others, signed a document calling for a military presence in Iraq and a retainment of it there to protect American interests, and now people are shocked that the Bush administration is planning on remaining in Iraq indefinitely. What do they think all the bases we're constructing are for?

____ Information technology is supposed to suppress tyranny by making society more open and people more accountable for their actions. Instead, all it’s doing is allowing us to watch exactly how the hegemons are seizing power. Technology can only act as a tool if we use it, and most people really don’t seem to care, or even prefer how things are progressing. The Soviet Union collapsed because people were able to see that capitalist nations were richer and the people there could afford luxury items. The Russians wanted that too. Now the Russian economy is stable and the people are wealthy. They see Putin as the source of this, and rather than being disgusted by his increasing stranglehold on Russian politics, they want him to stay in power. In the United States, as long as you still have your Sport Utility Vehicle, air conditioning, large house, and iPod to shut out the rest of the world, then who cares about civil liberties? They can have them so long as we keep getting the big paychecks. It’s not freedom people want; it’s luxury and stability.
