____ In regards to creating the psychology of the Joker for The Dark Knight, Christopher Nolan has stated that the thing he is most afraid of is "chaos and anarchy." The Joker is widely regarded as Batman's greatest nemesis, for he is both the complete opposite of what Batman symbolizes and the risk of what Bruce Wayne can always become. Whereas Wayne's response to a lawless world was to turn to vigilante justice in an attempt to restore order, the man who would become the Joker lost his sanity and became the epitome of a world he could not handle, at least as he is portrayed in Alan Moore's The Killing Joke. In response to "one bad day," Batman was born in order to symbolize order while the Joker came to symbolize chaos.

____ At the end of The Killing Joke, Batman offers to help the Joker get therapy and the Joker refuses, telling instead the story of two men in an asylum who escape to the roof. One jumps across the gap between the asylum and the next building and makes it to freedom while the second is too afraid. The first offers to shine his flashlight across the gap so the second can walk across the beam to which the latter responds, "No, no. What do you think I am, crazy? You'd turn the light off when I was halfway across." The two men are of course Batman and the Joker and the asylum is society. Bruce Wayne is able to succesfully escape the madness of the world by jumping across the abyss and seeking order in a meaningless universe while the Joker cannot face the blackness and instead goes insane. This is the path Bruce Wayne can always fall onto, thus the Joker represents the ultimate descent of Batman should he ever take justice to the point of killing.

____ As a clown, the Joker is the trickster archetype. In certain Amerindian tribes, individuals portraying this form had free reign to disrupt ceremonies and behave in any manner they wished, even being known to ride backwards into battle. A trickster could walk into the most sacred ritual and belch for he was a reminder that everything was a creation of the human mind and had no absolute significance in the grand scheme of the cosmos. It is the apparent law and order that our society holds dear which the Joker finds so hilarious. In the teaser trailer for Nolan's The Dark Knight, the Joker states, "Starting tonight, people will die. I'm a man of my word," and then bursts into a cackle. It is the cliche statement "I'm a man of my word," that brings him to fits; the idea that there is any purpose or reason to this world is absurd and is the greatest joke of all.

____ Of course, the Joker has been portrayed in various ways in DC comics, from a harmless prankster during the comic code years to a sane criminal hiding behind the mask of madness to the serious psychopath in his original incarnation and the latest representations. It is the latter version that I feel resounds the most meaningfully, allowing Batman and the Joker to transcend a mere hero and villain and become archetypes representing greater ideas in the human experience.
