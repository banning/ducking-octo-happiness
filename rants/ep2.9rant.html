____ According to legend, the bottom of Lake Tahoe in Nevada is covered in the frozen bodies of mob hits. The extreme depth (900+ feet) and cold have kept the unfortunate souls swimming with the fishes for decades. Jacques Cousteau is said to have been testing a submarine in the lake when he came upon something that terrified him. He claimed, "the world is not yet ready for what is down there." Some have speculated he saw "Tahoe Tessie," the lake monster of Lake Tahoe. Why a marine biologist would say such a thing about an aquatic animal seems rather strange. Frozen alien bodies seem like a more reasonable explanation for what Mr. Cousteau saw, at least to me. Unfortunately, Jacques left no footage or any other data from his dive behind. Some say this is because there's no evidence he ever dived in the lake at all and such stories are fictions. Even if there was a submarine expedition in Lake Tahoe, any victims of the mafia would have long been eaten by fish and crustaceans. Like all large bodies of water, Tahoe has its own legends and monsters hiding in the depths of the human imagination. There is something about an abyss that inspires people to dream of the fantastic. The spread of civilization and near destruction of the wilderness hasn't been able to rob us of the desire to project dangers into unexplored territory. We're constantly fashioning stories to explain our existence, whether about other people, blobs of light on garage doors, or what lurks beneath a lake. More water analysis when the characters arrive a large body of water. Which one could that be?
