____ Let's start looking at t-shirts! As seen more clearly in the previous comic, Fro's shirt features a seagull caught in a six-pack ring. Among others things plastic may make possible are cancer, diabetes, and feminization due to endocrine disruption. Essentially, various polycarbons that are used in everyday products such as can liners, bottles, car varnish, clothing, rugs, etcetera, have been shown to cause prostate cancer and obesity in mice. So we've spent decades wiping out deadly diseases that have plagued our species for millennia only to now face sickness from our own technology. Plastic germs for a better tomorrow. In regards to feminization, our industrial pollution has been releasing estrogens and estrogen mimicking molecules into the environment for decades, which so far have been found to be skewing sex ratios in fish and birds in highly contaminated areas towards females, and causing males to develop female sexual organs. Now it seems sex ratios among humans in Greenland and Russia are skewing 2:1 towards girls among people eating large amounts of sea food. In general, conservatives tend to oppose feminism and support laissez-faire capitalism, the latter of which cares more about maximizing the bottom line than conserving the environment. So our patriarchal society is ironically slowly turning itself female through its waste output. That's hilarious, and supports my fringe religion.

____ On top of possibly contributing to disease prevalence and increasing the number of girls over boys in future generations, plastic also gives us one of the largest found art pieces on the planet. As the Best Life article explains, the North Pacific Gyre is now home to a swirling patch of garbage around twice the size of Texas. That's plastic bags, traffic cones, bottles, kayaks, and an ever increasing number of tampon applicators drifting about, being consumed by seagulls and fish. And their component molecules (six times more plastic in the patch than plankton) just concentrate up the food chain to come right back to the people producing the waste. Oh, the hilarity of it all masks the horror.
