____ Guy's shirt features a revolver and the phrase "The Simplest Surreal Act." In the first Surrealist Manifesot, André Breton states "The simplest Surrealist act consists of dashing down into the street, pistol in hand, and firing blindly, as fast as you can pull the trigger, into the crowd." So I guess the French are to blame for all the school shootings and not Marilyn Manson, although he is a self-identified surrealist. I was going to link an article calling 9-11 the ultimate surreal act since I've already called string theory and the Great Pacific Garbage Patch art, but I'm unfortunately unable to locate it. The author summarizes the denouncement of the West and calls for its destruction by surrealist figureheads and their championing of irrationality and Oriental values. He also references the recurring theme of skyscrapers and plains seen in many paintings of the era. In citing this, I'm not saying the attacks on that day are a result of the surrealist movement, but that both are products of a twentieth century western civilization and its emphasis on the accumulation of capital. But whereas the surrealists only spoke of destruction in their rebellion against the norms of society, contemporary Muslim unrest with corporate expansion at the expense of human rights has flourished into violence, and leaders in the West have exploited this rage to consolidate their power over the masses.

____ One of the functions of art is to act as a catharsis and to help us comprehend our existence. Surrealism could only have been engendered out of the twentieth century milieu. The perceived failures of rationality in the face of world war and an oppressed working class spawned the denouncement of the values of the culture that produced such horrors. Violence is a reaction to one's surroundings whereas art is a reflection on them. The uniform description of 9-11 as "surreal" is indeed appropriate in light of the manifestoes of the 1920's as it was the literal response to the anxieties the surrealists were attempting to describe philosophically. And with the philosophy already in place, people turned to its ideas, now imbedded in our culture, to attempt to understand the horror they were witnessing. 9-11 could be considered the ultimate surreal act merely because that philosophy can describe that day and the events surrounding it so well and perhaps even calls for such attacks, but only because such thought has been engendered by the same cultural forces which precipitated the destruction of 9-11. The values of our civilization are the over-arching forces at play here, and the cataclysms they produce and the philosophies used to describe them are a sign of how sick things have become.
