____ I found an ankh at the gas station on Sunday, lying on the asphalt, covered in dirt and rain. It’s made of metal. I was very happy for finding it, but don’t count it as an omen of anything. While I like to believe synchronicities and signs are shadows of greater plans, I am also skeptical of any meaning to the Universe. Or if there is some metaphysical/paranormal force connecting objects, events, and people in a web work of meaning, it isn’t doing so out of guiding us to some destination, but merely responding to the information we project onto reality and reflecting back similar data in a case of spiritual super symmetry. Of course, there’s also the human nervous system’s ability to find patterns where in fact none exist, as has been chronicled in previous rants, although they leaned more towards pareidolia than apophenia. I am a mystic-nihilist the same way light is both a particle and a wave.

____ But back to that ankh. The cross with a handle is the traditional Egyptian symbol of life and over time has become associated with the eternality of such. According to Robert Heinlein in “I Will Fear No Evil,” it is also a phallus and a vulva and to give a person an ankh as a gift is to tell him/her, “I want to have sex with you.” These two interpretations of the symbol can be seen as one in the same. Sex is the key to eternal life, for it leads to reproduction and the generation of offspring. The cells of one’s body continue dividing in a new organism. The original cells may die and the original matter brought into the union may end up some place else, but the information, the pattern governing the organism continues on. Life continues.

____ To take the definition in English literally, the ankh is eternal life, not eternal ego. The self ceases to be, or maybe does not exist to begin with if Siddhartha, Hume, and modern neuroscience are to be believed.

____ Of course, if life is an eternal pattern molding itself onto whatever matter is at hand (Rupert Sheldrake’s morphic fields could enter in this ramble, but I’m trying not to stray too far out of accepted models of reality), consciousness could be the same, received by nervous systems whenever they arise as radio telescopes receive the sounds of stars. Thus the grand web work of the universe (a decentralized intelligence akin to the Internet in which ever part acts on every other part, as Robert Anton Wilson suspected near the end of his life) interprets the meanings we weave in our existence and reflects back the appropriate artifacts and events to lay hints of itself. But that sounds too intentional, too much like a personal god. No, synchronicities are just a property of the system, a feedback loop.

____ Life is eternal because it can reproduce, but self must die.
____ Life is eternal because there is no such thing as time and everything has already ended and has yet to begin.
____ Life is eternal because consciousness survives death.

____ These are all just scripts running on my nervous system, none more provable than the other. It is meaningless to speculate about anything outside of phenomenological experience.

____ (Yet every morning on the way to work I see a billboard for a housing community named “Walden.”)
